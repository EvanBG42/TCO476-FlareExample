History and Monuments

As the United States' capital city, Washington, DC has a storied history. The countless monuments in DC celebrate not only the city's heritage, but the history of our entire country. A vast majority of these monuments are located near the National Mall, so that's the primary place to be if you're interested in monuments and memorials.

Favorites On the National Mall

  • Lincoln Memorial
    • This memorial is dedicated to Abraham Lincoln and his accomplishments as president, including the Emancipation Proclamation, and helping to preserve the Union.
  • Washington Monument
    • This is a large obelisk style monument dedicated to the United States' first president, George Washington. The monument itself has a notable history and construction, taking nearly 40 years to finish construction.
  • Vietnam Veterans Memorial
    • The Vietnam Veterans Memorial is one of the most unique monuments in DC due to its unconventional design, and 3 separate parts. The parts are named the "Memorial Wall", the "Three Soldiers", and the "Vietnam Women's Memorial".

Favorites Around DC

  • Albert Einstein Memorial
    • This memorial is dedicated to Albert Einstein and the contributions he made to the human understanding of the universe.
  • Nuns of the Battlefield
    • This memorial is dedicated to the hundreds of nurses of helped the wounded on both sides of the Civil War.
